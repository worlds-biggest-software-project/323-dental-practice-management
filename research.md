## Relevant Industry Standards or Protocols

- **CDT (Current Dental Terminology) 2026** — ADA-maintained procedure code set updated annually; CDT 2026 introduced 31 new codes and 14 revisions, the most technology-focused update in recent years; all compliant software must be updated accordingly
- **ANSI/ADA Standards** — three new proposed standards were under review in 2026 covering electronic records, diagnostics, and treatment planning integration
- **HIPAA** — all patient records, radiographs, and billing data are covered health information requiring encryption, access controls, and audit trails
- **X12 837D / 835 EDI** — standard electronic claim submission and remittance advice formats used by all major dental insurers
- **DICOM** — standard for radiograph and imaging interoperability between digital X-ray equipment, cone-beam CT, and the practice management system
- **HL7 FHIR** — increasingly required for data exchange with medical EHRs, referral networks, and patient-facing health apps
- **NPI (National Provider Identifier)** — federal identifier that must appear correctly on all claims; software must maintain provider credential records

## Market Research

**Market Size:** The global dental practice management software market is valued at approximately USD 2.62–2.64 billion in 2026, with projections ranging from USD 4.16–5.33 billion by 2031–2035, growing at 7–11% CAGR. North America holds more than 53% of global share, with the US market alone estimated at over USD 1 billion.

**Funding:** Henry Schein and Patterson Companies dominate through long-established suites. Dental service organisation (DSO) consolidation is driving demand for cloud-native, multi-site platforms; Planet DDS and Curve Dental have attracted growth capital targeting the DSO segment.

**Pricing Landscape:** Enterprise solutions are quote-based; smaller cloud platforms start at $200–$500/month per location. Total cost of ownership including hardware, imaging integration, and training can reach $20,000–$80,000 in year one for a full-featured implementation.

**Key Buyer Personas:** Solo dentist owners (need simplicity and strong billing), group practices (need multi-provider scheduling and reporting), dental service organisations with 10+ locations (need centralised analytics and compliance management), specialist practices such as orthodontists and oral surgeons (need specialty-specific charting and imaging).

**Notable Trends:** Cloud-based deployment now accounts for more than 55% of market share. AI-powered radiograph analysis (e.g., VideaHealth) is emerging as a separate layer integrated with existing PMS. Vendors are embedding imaging, CAD/CAM, and AI analytics directly into clinical workflows. Patient communication and automated scheduling are major revenue levers.

## AI-Native Opportunity

- **AI radiograph analysis** integrated natively into the charting workflow to flag potential caries, bone loss, and pathology at time of capture, reducing missed findings and liability risk
- **Treatment plan acceptance optimisation** using conversational AI to explain proposed treatment in patient-friendly language, anticipate objections, and suggest financing options automatically
- **Automated CDT coding from clinical notes** that reads the provider's documentation and recommends the correct procedure codes, reducing coding errors and claim denials
- **Intelligent recall and hygiene scheduling** that predicts patient no-show risk and proactively adjusts reminder cadence and channel (SMS, email, phone) accordingly
- **Real-time insurance eligibility and fee schedule optimisation** that computes expected reimbursement at treatment plan creation time, enabling accurate patient financial estimates before any procedure begins
